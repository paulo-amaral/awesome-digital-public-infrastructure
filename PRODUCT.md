# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Policy decision-makers and technical practitioners in digital government: government CIO offices, UN agencies (UNDP, UNICEF, ITU), donors, and the engineers who implement DPI. They arrive from the GitHub awesome-list or a shared link, usually scanning for a specific resource (a framework, a country example, a sandbox) rather than reading linearly.

## Product Purpose

The GitHub Pages landing page (docs/index.html) is the curated front door to the awesome-digital-public-infrastructure list: it presents DPG domains, country DPI profiles, key frameworks, papers, toolkits, and field notes, and routes visitors to the full README or external resources. Success: a visitor finds a relevant, trustworthy resource in under a minute and cites or shares the list.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, credible, direct. The visual identity deliberately follows Brand Estonia (Aino typeface, Estonian Blue #0000f0, Tudu yellow accent) as a nod to X-Road's origin; this identity is fixed, not up for redesign. Tone is factual and sourced, never promotional.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (gradient heroes, hero-metric templates, glassmorphism).
- Consultancy brochure sites: vague claims, stock imagery, buzzwords.
- Dark-mode-with-neon "AI tool" aesthetics; the page is a light, print-like editorial surface.

## Design Principles

1. Content is the interface: hierarchy, typography, and scannability beat decoration.
2. Preserve the Brand Estonia identity; polish happens inside it, never against it.
3. Every claim on the page is verifiable; awards, dates, and counts must match sources.
4. Fast and dependency-light: a single static HTML file with self-hosted fonts should stay that way.
5. Respect the reader's scan: consistent card and link patterns, no surprise layouts per section.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 body contrast, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, reduced-motion alternatives for any animation. Audience includes government users on old hardware and slow connections; performance is an accessibility concern.
