# Submitting to the Official Awesome List

This document tracks the process for submitting this list to [sindresorhus/awesome](https://github.com/sindresorhus/awesome).

---

## Before You Submit

The awesome list maintainers review submissions carefully. To improve the chances of getting accepted:

- [ ] The repo has at least 20–30 stars (share on LinkedIn first)
- [ ] At least 1–2 external contributions exist — ask colleagues to open a small PR
- [ ] The CI link checker is running and passing (check the Actions tab)
- [ ] `CONTRIBUTING.md` is complete and clear
- [ ] The repo has been public for at least 30 days

---

## How to Submit

1. Go to [github.com/sindresorhus/awesome](https://github.com/sindresorhus/awesome)
2. Fork the repository
3. In the fork, open `readme.md` and find the right section — likely **Miscellaneous** or a relevant tech category
4. Add this line in alphabetical order within the section:

```
- [Digital Public Infrastructure](https://github.com/paulo-amaral/awesome-digital-public-infrastructure) - Open-source tools, frameworks, and country implementations for governments and developers working on inclusive digital societies.
```

5. Open a pull request using the title and body below
6. Wait — the review process typically takes 2 to 8 weeks

## PR Body

```markdown
## Add awesome-digital-public-infrastructure

**Repository:** https://github.com/paulo-amaral/awesome-digital-public-infrastructure

**Description:** A list of Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) and Digital Public Goods (DPG) — open-source tools, frameworks, country implementations, and resources for governments, UN agencies, NGOs, and developers building inclusive digital societies.

---

### Why this list belongs here

**Fills a real gap.** There is no existing awesome-list covering Digital Public Infrastructure or Digital Public Goods. This is one of the fastest-growing areas in global tech policy — 50+ countries are actively implementing DPI, there are 140+ certified DPGs — and no curated resource consolidates them.

**What's in it:**
- Six domain categories of certified open-source DPGs: Health, Identity, Payments, Education, Climate, Data Exchange
- Country implementation profiles with field-verified data — India, Estonia, Brazil, Timor-Leste, and more
- Frameworks from UNDP, World Bank, OECD, ITU, and GovStack
- Assessment toolkits, learning resources, and job boards for the sector
- A Field Notes section with insights from real UN deployments — not found in any other list

**Who it's for:** Developers building DPG solutions, government digital teams, UN and multilateral organizations, international development consultants, GovTech practitioners, researchers, and policymakers.

**Maintained by:** A digital transformation consultant with field experience working with UNICEF, UNDP, and national government teams across fragile states and the Pacific.
```

## After Submitting

- Watch the PR for review comments — maintainers often ask for small adjustments
- If requested, update the entry description or move it to a different section
- Do not close and reopen the PR unless asked to — just push commits to the fork branch

---

## Notes

The DPI/DPG space moves fast. Keep the list updated while the PR is under review — an active repo with recent commits makes a better impression than a static one.
